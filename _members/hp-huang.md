Hsiang-Po Huang is a Professor at the Graduate Institute of Medical Genomics and Proteomics, College of Medicine, National Taiwan University. His research focuses on stem cells and cancer, induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs), membrane-bound proteases, and functional genomics.

#### Education

- Ph.D., Department of Molecular and Cellular Biology, Baylor College of Medicine, USA
- M.S., Graduate Institute of Pathology, National Taiwan University
- M.D., School of Medicine, National Taiwan University

#### Experience

- 2025–present: Professor, Graduate Institute of Medical Genomics and Proteomics, National Taiwan University
- 2020–2025: Associate Professor, Graduate Institute of Medical Genomics and Proteomics, National Taiwan University
- 2013–2020: Assistant Professor, Graduate Institute of Medical Genomics and Proteomics, National Taiwan University
- 2005–2013: Research Physician, Department of Medical Research, National Taiwan University Hospital
- 2000–2005: Resident, Department of Pediatrics, National Taiwan University Hospital
- 2000: Postdoctoral Fellow, Department of Molecular and Cellular Biology, Baylor College of Medicine, USA
