Yeu-Guang Tung is a medical student at National Taiwan University School of Medicine (class of 2020). As an amateur researcher, he enjoys writing papers. As an amateur engineer, he enjoys developing software. As an amateur composer, he enjoys creating music. As a professional student, he enjoys learning medicine, physics, and algorithms.

#### Highlights

- 2019: International Physics Olympiad Silver Medal

#### Selected Works

- **Comprehensive Atlas of Cancer-Type-Specific Molecular Features from Comparative Analysis of TCGA Data** (October 2025) — Analyzed TCGA data spanning 39 cancer types and 6 molecular platforms to create a comprehensive atlas of cancer-type-specific molecular features, covering somatic mutations, copy number variations, DNA methylation, transcriptome profiling, and multiomics clustering.

- **AMATERASU: Automated Mapping And Transcriptome Expression Research Analysis Suite** (December 2024) — Developed a command-line toolkit that streamlines RNA sequencing data analysis from raw data processing to advanced statistical analysis, combining alignment, expression profiling, clustering, differential expression, gene set enrichment, and survival analysis into a unified workflow.

- **Predicting Electron Distributions in Drug Molecules Using Density Functional Theory in Real Space** (November 2023) — Proposed a comprehensive scheme to perform DFT calculations in real space, demonstrating highly parallel computation using PyTorch and NumPy on the drug acyclovir.
